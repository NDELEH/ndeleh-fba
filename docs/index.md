The Ndeleh Fish Bone Algorithm is a new form of associative search that mimics biological processes of memory and recall. It works by building:

- A dynamic **spine** (main associative path)
- **Ribs** (secondary associations)
- **Micro-ribs** (weaker, deeper associations)
- An overall **morphology** describing the shape of the graph

N-FBA adapts its structure based on the data, producing shapes such as:

- linear
- rib-heavy
- clustered
- curved
- sparse

This adaptive structure is what makes N-FBA unique.
